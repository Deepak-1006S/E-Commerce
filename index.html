The Aurum Story

Born in the
silence of
Grasse

Founded in 2008 by master perfumer Élise Fontaine, Aurum was conceived as an act of refusal — a rejection of the mass-produced, the algorithmic, the safe. Every fragrance in the collection took no fewer than three years to compose, and will never be produced in more than 5,000 units.

Source Materials

Only the Rarest

01
Agarwood Oud
Harvested from wild Aquilaria trees in Assam. Aged a minimum of 40 years before distillation. Less than 200kg produced annually for Aurum.
02
Centifolia Rose
Hand-picked at dawn in May from the Domaine de Manon in Grasse. Each kilogram of absolute requires 4 tonnes of petals.
03
Mysore Sandalwood
Sustainably sourced from government-certified trees in Karnataka. Trees must be at least 30 years old before harvest begins.
04
Orris Butter
Iris rhizomes aged for three years in the open air before cold-pressing. Produces just 0.1% by weight of its raw material in precious butter.
